Affordable Housing

A safe, stable home is the foundation for everything else.

Research & News
Our Vision
“

Without stable housing, everything else cracks.

For too many Hawaiʻi residents, the foundation that stable housing provides is cracking. We face the highest housing costs in the country and one of the nation’s worst rates of homelessness. Only about 20% of residents can afford to buy a home. Median rents hover around $2,500 — roughly 50% higher than the national average — and nearly two-thirds of renter households spend more than 30% of their income on rent.

This is not just a housing crisis. It is a health crisis, an education crisis, and an economic crisis.

Housing policy must reflect local values: safe, accessible homes for all, regardless of income or circumstance. That means recognizing shelter as a basic human right. Our affordable housing work sits at the intersection of every other issue area — without a place to live, you cannot access food, transportation, education, or economic opportunity. Housing is the base. Everything else is built on top of it.

A market that leaves families behind

A minimum-wage worker would need to work 141 hours per week — about three and a half full-time jobs — to afford the average Fair Market Rent for a two-bedroom home in Hawaiʻi. Since 2000, the cost to purchase a home has grown over 230%, while rents have climbed twice as fast as wages.

Why this happens

Decades of artificial scarcity have outpaced wages.

Since 2000, the cost of purchasing a home in Hawaiʻi has risen over 230%. Rents have climbed roughly twice as fast as wages, and the gap has only widened. Restrictive zoning, slow permitting, and limited urban land use have throttled supply — while the cost of essentials keeps climbing. The math no longer works for working families.

2026 Legislative Priorities

Build, protect, and invest

Seven housing priorities, grouped into three areas where Hawaiʻi can change course — unlocking supply, keeping families housed, and funding affordability at scale.

1

Supply

Land-use & zoning reform

Decades of restrictive land-use rules have throttled construction. Hawaiʻi needs to build up instead of out — infilling existing neighborhoods, prioritizing housing near services and transit, and removing artificial caps that prevent housing within the urban land use boundary.

  • Maximize TOD density High minimum-density requirements in transit-oriented districts and removal of barriers to dense housing near transit. More people near goods, services, and transportation — lower costs and natural long-term affordability.
  • Reduce minimum lot sizes Require counties to reduce and standardize minimum lot sizes so smaller, more flexible homes are viable. Hawaiʻi’s land is more valuable than the houses on it — large lot minimums inflate the base cost of every home.
  • Inclusionary zoning reform Require counties to commission a study before implementing inclusionary zoning, so well-meaning programs don’t inadvertently raise housing costs or chill development.

2

Protect

Tenant rights & anti-displacement

Two-thirds of Hawaiʻi renters are cost-burdenedCost-burdened — spending more than 30% of household income on rent and utilities. Severely cost-burdened means spending over 50%., paying more than 30% of their income on rent. Understanding your rights is the first line of defense against displacement — and codifying existing best practices closes the gap between policy and reality.

  • Notice of tenant rights Require the Office of Consumer Protection to publish language-accessible notices of tenant rights, and require landlords to provide that notice to tenants. Understanding your rights is the best way to exercise them.
  • Displacement assistance Provide payment to renters displaced by new developments. HHFDC already does this in practice — this proposal encodes it in law so the protection applies statewide.

3

Invest

Fund affordability at scale

A modernized conveyance taxConveyance tax — a one-time tax paid when real property changes ownership. Hawaiʻi’s current rates top out at 1.25% for properties over $10M — well below peer-state luxury rates. on luxury real estate could generate over $100 million annually for affordable housing and Native Hawaiian home lands — without raising costs for the average owner-occupant. Pair it with deed-restriction tools that keep new housing available to local families.

  • Conveyance tax reform A marginal-rate structure that reduces taxes for average owner-occupants while increasing them on second properties and luxury homes. Generates over $100 million per year for infrastructure, affordable housing, and DHHL.
  • Locals-only housing Deed restrictions that reserve new housing for qualified residents at price points tied to local wages — via the 201H alternative pathway, ADU grants in exchange for deed restrictions, and county deed-restriction programs.

A Record of Results

Our Impact

A decade of housing wins — from rental assistance during the pandemic to permanent investments in the state's affordable housing pipeline.

2007 – 2008

Won the right to education for homeless keiki

Sued to ensure that homeless children in Hawaiʻi have full, meaningful access to a public education — closing the gap between policy and practice for the most vulnerable students in the state.

2008 – 2015

Forced overdue repairs to public housing

Sued to compel long-overdue upgrades at the Kuhio Park Terrace and Mayor Wright Homes public-housing developments, which had fallen into extreme disrepair. The litigation ended squalor conditions and won livable homes for thousands of low-income residents.

2018 – 2020

Preserved Front Street Apartments in Lahaina

Sued to preserve 51 years of affordable housing at Front Street Apartments, winning a judgment that the developer could not break its promise and raise rents to market rate decades early. The victory preserved 142 affordable units for low-income kūpuna and families — units that would later be destroyed in the 2023 wildfires.

2020 – 2022

Stood up Hawaiʻi’s pandemic rent-relief program

Advocated successfully for an emergency rent relief and eviction-mediation program during the COVID-19 pandemic. Our research later showed the program saved tens of thousands of households from homelessness when Hawaiʻi unemployment was among the highest in the nation.

2022

Secured ~$1B in state housing funds

Helped secure nearly $1 billion in state funds for affordable housing development — including $600 million for the Department of Hawaiian Home Lands, the largest single appropriation for DHHL in state history.

2023 – Present

Leading conveyance tax reform & tenant protections

Driving the fight for conveyance-tax reform that generates over $100 million annually for affordable housing and Native Hawaiian home lands. Advocating for tenant-protection bills that give renters the information and stability they need to stay housed, and for regulatory reforms that remove artificial caps on housing within the urban land use boundary.
