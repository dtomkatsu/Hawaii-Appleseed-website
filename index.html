Our work — in four areas

Changing systems to address inequality and foster greater opportunity.

Hawaiʻi Appleseed is a nonpartisan, nonprofit law and policy center. We research the conditions holding local families back and advocate for the rules, budgets, and programs that can change them — at the Capitol, in the courts, and in community.

Four areas where policy can make Hawaiʻi affordable again.

Each of our issue areas addresses a cost squeezing local families — and each has concrete policy levers at the state and county level.

A home everyone can afford, on the islands they call home.

Hawaiʻi has the highest housing costs in the country and one of the nation's worst rates of homelessness. We push for zoning reform, public financing for permanently affordable homes, and stronger tenant protections to keep kūpuna and keiki housed.

Getting to work, school, and the beach — without a car payment.

A tax code that works for working families.

Hawaiʻi has one of the most regressive tax structures in the country. We champion an expanded Earned Income Tax Credit, a refundable child tax credit, and reforms that stop taxing families into poverty.
